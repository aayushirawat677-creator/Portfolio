01 · The Problem

Working backwards from the customer.

"I want a cover for my MacBook Air." Rufus showed me a shelf of cases based on what I'd browsed before. It guessed. It guessed wrong.

It didn't know my laptop size, my budget, whether I needed pockets, or that I just wanted a slim sleeve to slip inside my backpack. I went to Google, found my answer on a Reddit thread, and came back to Amazon to buy it manually. Rufus was absent from the most important moment in my journey — the moment I was trying to decide.

The root cause: Rufus builds recommendations from three passive signals — order history, browsing history, and the keyword you typed. All three describe what you did before, not what you need right now. So two people asking the exact same question get the same generic answer, even when their needs are completely different.

04 · How It Works

Ask what you don't yet know.

Before recommending anything, Rufus asks itself: "What don't I know about this person's situation that would change my recommendation?" Those gaps become the questions.

1
Never ask what the user already told you"Cheap MacBook cover" → budget is answered. Move on.
2
Ask only what changes the recommendationIrrelevant questions erode trust. Every question must have a purpose.
3
Never block the userEvery question has a Skip option. Rufus gives its best answer even with incomplete information.

06 · Dimensional Intelligence

Tag matching is not fit verification.

The insight that separates Smart Clarification from every existing shopping assistant.

For any product where physical fit matters — laptop covers, phone cases, furniture, clothing, cookware — Rufus shouldn't rely on a tag like "13-inch compatible." It should reason about actual dimensions and real-world constraints.

// MacBook Air 13" = 11.97 × 8.36 in cover tagged "13-inch compatible" → internal cavity 13.2 × 9.1 in without pockets → fits (0.23 in clearance each side) but user needs pockets → front-pocket stitching compresses width 0.3–0.8 in 13.2 − 0.6 compression → 12.6 in effective → laptop won't slide in → return // Rufus rule if (needs_pockets) min_internal = 13.5 × 9.5 in → auto-filter, then explain why

Rufus discloses the exclusion in plain language: "I've removed covers that would be too tight once pockets are factored in — here's what will actually fit." No current shopping tool does this.

07 · Conversation Example

The MacBook Air cover, annotated.

Six unknowns, asked most-consequential first. Every step shows Rufus's reasoning.

Q1

"Which MacBook Air do you have?"

user: 13-inch

A wrong size = a wrong product, full stop — so size goes first. Records 11.97 × 8.36 in as the compatibility anchor for every cover considered.

Q2

"Material preference?" → "Budget?"

user: fabric · under $50

Budget is most likely to reshape the product set next; material narrows the field. Hard filters, applied early.

Q3

"Do you need pockets, or a clean sleeve?"

user: pockets — charger + cables

The critical branch. Pockets confirmed → activates the dimensional filter: minimum internal cavity 13.5 × 9.5 in. Every cover below that is excluded, regardless of its "13-inch compatible" tag.

Q4

"Carry on its own, or inside a bag?"

user: inside a backpack — just a sleeve

Profile complete. Handles, straps and rigid structure excluded. Rufus searches catalogue + expert sources and returns 5 confirmed-to-fit sleeves — each with a one-line reason and source ("Recommended by Wirecutter as best fabric sleeve for MacBook Air").

08 · Functional Requirements

What engineering builds.

FR-01 / 02

Intent gaps & progressive questions

Parse the query for category, stated constraints, and unknown attributes. Generate questions dynamically — never a hardcoded per-category script. Max 5; viable recommendation after 2.

FR-03

Dimensional reasoning

Retrieve verified dimensions where fit matters; apply real-world compatibility logic (pocket compression, inseam, clearance); disclose and explain every exclusion.

FR-04

Multi-source engine

Search catalogue + external experts in parallel. Rank by attribute match, expert endorsement, then review quality. Sponsored items are labelled and may not displace top organic matches.

FR-05

Post-rec refinement

Offer cheaper / premium / brand / material filters without restarting. Any prior answer can change at any time, context retained.

FR-06

Session memory

All answers persist for the session; returning keeps them as defaults. Rufus acknowledges prior answers: "Since you mentioned fabric earlier…"

// non-functional

Speed & access

<300ms question render, <1.5s post-answer results, <15min catalogue freshness. Full voice parity, WCAG 2.1 AA, session-only storage in v1.

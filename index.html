AAYUSHI · PRODUCT MANAGER

I build the agents people actually keep talking to.

I'm Aayushi — an AI Product Manager with 3.5 years taking LLM-driven agents from blank page to live product. Most recently I co-founded and led product for Lumi, a sleep-focused behavioral AI voice agent that calls users and coaches them through getting started. I validated demand with Wizard-of-Oz before writing production code — 83% of participants said they'd keep using it, 90 NPS, $40/mo validated willingness to pay — then shipped the live iOS product, which drove +40% DAU in early usage.

My edge is that I think in tradeoffs, not features. I can decide what an agent should reason about and what stays deterministic, define what "good" actually means with real AI-quality metrics — accuracy, tone, helpfulness — and set the constraints engineers build against: where to spend latency, where to spend cost, where safety can't bend. On Lumi that meant making the live call never-freeze non-negotiable; on What If? it meant choosing deterministic pattern-matching over LLM judgment for jailbreak-resistant safety stops.

I don't just spec agents — I build them. What If? (Microsoft Agents League 2026) is a multi-agent decision tool I built solo on Azure AI Foundry: a 3-stage, 4-agent pipeline that turns an open-ended decision into two grounded outcome timelines, with web-search grounding, a 7-dimension LLM-as-judge eval framework, and deterministic safety guardrails for crisis routing and age-gating. Working prototype, design to deployed code — and a clean demonstration of my core principle: let the LLM do synthesis, keep judgment and safety in deterministic code.

What ties it together: IIT-Kharagpur systems rigor, a Harvard management lens, and an operator's bias for shipping — across health, voice AI, commerce and telemedicine, in three countries. Off the clock I'm a relentless home cook, I run long, I collect stones from places that meant something, and I get lost in decision-science books.

01 · Intro

Not another to-do list — a personal AI behavioral coach.

Lumi is an AI behavioral coach powered by Gemini 3. It doesn't just remind you — it accompanies you through the whole process until action happens.

Traditional productivity tools — alarms, to-do lists — are open-loop systems. They fire a reminder and assume you'll act. For the ADHD brain that assumption breaks: the problem was never "not knowing what to do," it's "being unable to start." So reminders pile into alarm fatigue and a quiet sense of shame.

Lumi flips the model into a closed loop — it senses your state, compares reality against the goal, and dynamically adjusts its intervention until the gap between "I want to do it" and "I did it" actually closes.

04 · How It Works

Three stages, one continuous loop.

01

Perception & Planning

goal store · 3-dimensional memory · AI goal decomposition

A multi-dimensional context system reads your original goal ("I want to go to bed early") against three databases: Interaction Memory (past conversations & contracts), Behavior Log (real-time execution records), and Physiological Data (objective body state via Apple Health). Gemini 3 then acts as a planner, decomposing the ambitious goal into tiny, low-resistance atomic actions: 22:00 receive reminder → 22:15 put down the phone → 22:25 brush teeth → 22:40 in bed, wind down.

02

Tools & Environment

behavior-change methodology, encoded as AI-callable tools

Once it understands you, Lumi has to do something to drive action. Each Atomic Habits principle is encoded as an intervention mechanism the agent can invoke — from a proactive phone call to dimming your lights (detailed below).

03

Feedback Loop

reasoning · reflection · dynamic replanning

The core brain. Lumi reflects on subjective feeling (Ripple Memory), objective behavior (Task Log) and physiological data (Apple Health), then decides whether to push harder or back off — and rewrites the plan accordingly.

05 · AI Architecture

A 3-layer architecture that stays fast and deep.

Decoupling conversation from execution — the design decision that made real-time voice + complex reasoning coexist.

L Lumi
3-Layer Architecture
L1PerceptionGemini Live · Flash

Real-time voice + emotional support over a persistent WebSocket. Ultra-fast response; deliberately avoids heavy tool calls to stay connected.

L2Intent RouterGemini 3 Flash

Rapidly analyzes conversational context and decides whether the user's intent requires an external tool call.

L3Execution & PlanningGemini 3

Complex analysis, structured DB read/write, and dynamic context injection via a parallel-running AI.

context // Interaction MemoryBehavior LogApple Health
Why three layers

Decoupling solved the disconnect problem.

conversation layer ✕ execution layer

The first build tried to run tool calls directly through the Gemini Live stream — but complex function calling kept disconnecting the real-time model, with poor execution stability. The fix was to split perception (L1) from routing (L2) and execution (L3), and introduce a parallel AI for dynamic memory injection. Conversation stays sub-second and stable; heavy reasoning happens out of band.

06 · Intervention Tools

Five tools that turn intent into action.

Each one is a behavior-change principle, encoded as an AI-callable mechanism.

// tool 01

Proactive VoIP Call

The first AI that calls you. A real-time call is far harder to dismiss than a push notification — Lumi senses hesitation mid-call and gently interrupts the procrastination like a real coach.

// tool 02

App Lockdown + Voice Commitment

Keep scrolling and Lumi uses the iOS Screen Time API to lock temptation apps. To unlock, you speak a consequence statement aloud — verified by Azure Speech. Not punishment: forced metacognition.

// tool 03

Body Double Mode

Validated ADHD strategy — digital companionship for activation. Lumi suspends the costly voice model for white-noise animation, then performs a "hot reconnection" with full context the moment you speak, saving ~90% of token cost.

// tool 04

HomeKit Environment Control

Design the environment, not the willpower. Via Apple HomeKit, Lumi auto-dims your smart lights to a warm tone during wind-down — an environmental signal that says "it's time to rest."

// tool 05

Multimodal Visual Verification

Trust, but verify. Claim you brushed your teeth and Gemini's multimodal vision confirms — e.g. a photo of the toothbrush — before granting gamified coin rewards that reinforce the loop.

07 · Reasoning Engine

Detective-style reasoning, in four steps.

The feedback loop's brain investigates every gap like a case.

01

Gap Analysis

Why don't the user's "goal card" and "reality snapshot card" match up right now?

02

Root-Cause Diagnosis

Use physiological data — heart rate, sleep — to tell whether it's "too tired" (physiological) or "procrastinating" (psychological).

03

Decision Generation

Based on the diagnosis, choose the strategy: keep pushing, or suggest rest?

04

Dynamic Replanning

Translate the vague goal into a specific instruction: "It's 10:15 — put down your phone and start meditating." A weekly cross-data report surfaces causal patterns ("Friday's social event caused your sleep regression").

01 · Intro

Not another reminder — an AI that coaches you to the start line.

Lumi is a sleep-focused behavioral AI voice agent. It doesn't just remind you to wind down — it calls you, walks you through getting started, and adjusts based on how the night is actually going.

Traditional productivity tools — alarms, to-do lists — are open-loop systems. They fire a reminder and assume you'll act. But for the people Lumi is built for, that assumption breaks: the problem was never "not knowing what to do," it's "being unable to start." So reminders pile into alarm fatigue and a quiet sense of shame.

Lumi closes the loop — it senses your state, compares reality against the goal, and adjusts its intervention until the gap between "I want to sleep on time" and "I actually did" starts to close.

Where this goes: sleep is where Lumi was built and validated, but the same closed-loop approach extends to broader executive-function support — the longer-term thesis, not today's scope.

02 · The Problem

The intention–execution gap.

Knowing what to do, and being unable to start, are two different problems. Lumi is built for the second one.

// pain 01

Can't initiate

The hard part isn't knowing you should sleep — it's putting the phone down and actually beginning the wind-down. Willpower alone doesn't bridge that gap.

// pain 02

Alarm fatigue

Open-loop reminders are the easiest notification to dismiss. Over time they stop working entirely — and breed avoidance.

// pain 03

Shame spiral

Each ignored reminder reinforces a sense of personal failure, making the next attempt even harder to begin.

03 · The Solution

Closed-loop control, borrowed from cybernetics.

Two disciplines, encoded directly into the product.

// foundation

Atomic Habits

James Clear's four laws — make the cue obvious, the habit attractive, the action easy, the reward satisfying — become Lumi's intervention "tool layer."

// foundation

Cybernetics

Wiener's feedback loop: a thermostat holds temperature not with one command, but by continuously sensing, comparing to target, and re-adjusting. So does Lumi.

// core paradigm — Human-as-Tool
Most agents: human → instructs AI → AI calls software tools.
Lumi reverses it: AI orchestrates → calls the human as an API to execute a physical action.
tool.sleep_routine.start() → guide user to dim lights, brush, sleep
04 · How It Works

Three stages, one continuous loop.

01

Perception & Planning

goal store · multi-source memory · AI goal decomposition

A multi-source context system reads your original goal ("I want to go to bed early") against three signals: Interaction Memory (past conversations & commitments), Behavior Log (real-time execution records), and Physiological Data (objective body state via Apple Health). The planning model then decomposes the ambitious goal into tiny, low-resistance atomic actions: 22:00 receive call → 22:15 put down the phone → 22:25 brush teeth → 22:40 in bed, wind down.

02

Tools & Environment

behavior-change methodology, encoded as AI-callable tools

Once it understands you, Lumi has to do something to drive action. Each Atomic Habits principle is encoded as an intervention mechanism the agent can invoke — from a proactive phone call to dimming your lights (detailed below).

03

Feedback Loop

reasoning · reflection · dynamic replanning

The core brain. Lumi reflects on subjective feeling, objective behavior (Task Log) and physiological data (Apple Health), then decides whether to push harder or back off — and rewrites the plan accordingly.

The three design decisions

Where a voice agent actually breaks.

Decision 01 — decouple voice from reasoning

Heavy tool calls were killing the live connection.

The first build ran complex function calls directly through the live voice stream — and the calls kept disconnecting the real-time model. The fix: split real-time voice (L1) from routing (L2) and execution (L3), so heavy reasoning happens out of band. Conversation stays sub-second; the line never drops mid-intervention.

Decision 02 — steer behavior mid-session

A live session's instructions can't be changed once set.

To shift the agent's behavior in real time — from coaching to listening the instant a user says something vulnerable — I designed a parallel virtual-message channel: a priority queue (EMPATHY > DIRECTIVE > CHECKPOINT > CONTEXT) that injects runtime guidance as text the model treats as system instruction and never speaks aloud. It's how memory and empathy get injected without restarting the call.

Decision 03 — control cost with VAD

Keeping a live voice model open during silence is expensive.

A voice-activity-detection wake/sleep design drops the live connection during quiet stretches and re-establishes it on speech — cutting real-time voice cost materially while preserving the "always there" feel.

06 · Intervention Tools

Behavior-change principles, encoded as callable tools.

Each one turns a known habit-formation technique into something the agent can actually invoke.

// tool 01

Proactive voice call

The wedge: an AI that calls you. A real-time call is far harder to dismiss than a push notification — Lumi senses hesitation mid-call and gently nudges past the procrastination, like a coach would.

// tool 02

App lockdown + voice commitment

Keep scrolling and Lumi uses the iOS Screen Time API to lock temptation apps. To unlock, you speak a consequence statement aloud. Not punishment — forced metacognition.

// tool 03

Body double mode

A validated focus strategy: companionship for activation. Lumi suspends the costly voice model for ambient audio, then hot-reconnects with full context the moment you speak — the same VAD design that controls cost. Full PRD →

// tool 04

HomeKit environment control

Design the environment, not the willpower. Via Apple HomeKit, Lumi auto-dims smart lights to a warm tone during wind-down — an environmental cue that says "it's time to rest."

// tool 05

Multimodal visual verification

Trust, but verify. Claim you brushed your teeth and a vision model confirms — e.g. a photo of the toothbrush — before granting the reward that reinforces the loop.

07 · Reasoning Engine

Detective-style reasoning, in four steps.

When the plan and reality don't match, the loop investigates the gap like a case — instead of just logging a failure.

01

Gap analysis

Why don't the user's goal and their current reality match up right now?

02

Root-cause diagnosis

Use physiological signals — heart rate, sleep — to tell whether it's "too tired" (physiological) or "procrastinating" (psychological). Two very different responses.

03

Decision generation

Based on the diagnosis, choose the strategy: keep pushing, or back off and suggest rest?

04

Dynamic replanning

Translate the vague goal into a specific instruction: "It's 10:15 — put down your phone and start winding down." A weekly cross-data report surfaces patterns ("Friday social events keep pushing your sleep later").

09 · North Star

The metric I actually watch: Conversation-to-Action.

For a product like this, DAU can lie. So I designed the metric around the behavior that proves real value.

// why DAU isn't enough

A user who talks to Lumi for 30 minutes and starts nothing isn't a win — that's a companion bot, not a behavior tool. Engagement time can climb while the product quietly fails at its only job. So the number I optimize is whether the conversation actually ends in action.

Conversation-to-Action Did the user, within minutes of talking to Lumi, actually begin the task? > chosen over raw session time
Resurrection rate When a user breaks their habit, do they come back — without shame — instead of disappearing? > chosen over unbroken streaks

For an executive-function product, return-after-a-lapse is a truer signal of value than a streak — because the users who need it most are exactly the ones who will lapse. A product they're willing to return to, at their lowest moment, is one that earned trust.

10 · Guardrails

Data-driven empathy — when to push, when to let go.

Forceful intervention can't feel like a nagging parent. The guardrails are what make it feel like a coach. (Early user research killed an explicit "tough-love" persona — pressure-based coaching triggered avoidance and uninstalls.)

✓
Source-of-truth overridePhysiological data decides the call. Genuine exhaustion activates override rules (suggest rest); psychological procrastination keeps the strategy gently pushing.
✓
Metacognition, not punishmentThe voice-commitment unlock forces a conscious choice — making the cost explicit instead of crudely blocking. Educational, not adversarial.
✓
Verify before rewardRewards are only granted after verification — closing the gap between "I did it" claims and actual completion.
✓
Stability over capabilityHeavy tool calls are kept off the real-time layer so the agent never disconnects mid-intervention. (Decision 01, enforced as a rule.)
